The Analysis and Prediction Tool for COVID-19 Cases can be reached via the following link:


https://iuk-forecast.uniklinik-freiburg.de/


The website opens in English by default.


A progress bar appears in the bottom right-hand corner of the screen (Fetching RKI data…).


After uploading the current data of the Robert-Koch-Institut and the Register of the German Interdisciplinary Association for Intensive Care and Emergency Medicine (DIVI Intensive Care Registry: Deutsche Interdisziplinäre Vereinigung für Intensiv- und Notfallmedizin, DIVI-Intensivregister) the language will automatically switch to German. The application is now ready for use.


To change the language click on the Flag.


The user interface consists of a control panel (on the left) and a results panel (on the right).


In the smartphone application, the panels are arranged in vertical order.

The results panel has seven tabs along the upper margin.


To run the analysis and prediction tool, we recommend using the first four tabs (from left to right), starting with Incidence. In the following, we will introduce you to all functions in that order.



Incidence


In the control panel (left side)


Choose a state from the drop down menu and enter the district(s) of the catchment area for which you wish to make a prediction.


Optionally, checking the box “Select all states/Select all districts” also allows for national or state-wide predictions.


The selected states and districts will be checked with a tick in the corresponding fields of the drop down menu.


Population shows the population size in the selected catchment area as reported by the authorities.


ICU bed occupancy (DIVI registry) provides the number of ICU beds currently occupied by Covid-19 patients in the selected areas.


The Forecast start date corresponds to the last calendar day for which RKI data are available (usually the previous day). This can be backdated as desired.


The Forecast length (days) and the Number of runs can be set and selectively changed. In the default settings, the forecast length is set to 30 days and the number of simulations to five runs.



Caution! If the computational demand is too high, e.g. nationwide computations with forecasts beyond 182 days (corresponding to half a year) and more than five runs, a warning (limiter) will be displayed and the values automatically return to the default setting.


In the results panel (right side):


The result of your search will appear. The blue line in the graph represents the epidemiological curve of cases reported for each calendar day. The scale is on the left y-axes.


The black line illustrates the seven-day incidence (per 100,000 inhabitants) for each calendar day. The scale is on the right y-axes.


By using the slider beneath the chart, calendar periods can be narrowed down to improve resolution and visibility.


The blue field beneath the slider shows the number of newly reported infections.


The grey field beneath the slider shows the current seven-day incidence.


The green field shows the proportion of fully (second dose) vaccinated people.



Effective R-value (EpiEstim)


The figure illustrates the time-dependent effective R-value for each calendar day.

The vertical grey line corresponds to the date of the most recent R-value (previous day). This value is also rounded to two decimal points in the grey field.


To compensate for the RKI-dependent reporting delay, a Nowcast R-value is specified. This serves as the baseline for the forward predictions.


The Nowcast estimate compensates for delays in the reporting chain. This is achieved by a stochastic simulation of the delay-specific probability distribution. The effect becomes visible by zooming in on the last calendar days using the slider function.


By optionally changing the Forecast start date in the control panel (left side), the effective R-value for each previous calendar day can be called up. This date is then indicated by a vertical ochre line in the progression chart and in the field with the same colour underneath (set the slider accordingly).


Caution! If the forecast start date is set to more than 30 days in the past, you must adjust the forecast period in the control panel (the default is set to 30 days) for the present dynamics to remain visible.


Optional: Include the effect of Variants of Concern (VoC) in the right lower corner

Activating this function allows to predict the displacement dynamics of VoC.

Enter an historical date for which the proportion of VoC is known.


In the next field, enter the proportion of VoC determined on that date.


In the bottom field, enter the relative fitness advantage of the VoC (relative increase in transmissibility). In the default setting, the relative fitness advantage is set to 50%.



In the figure a red line appears above and a blue line below the recorded R-value estimates (black line).


The red line indicates the expected R-values, were the VoC proportion to make up 100% of the circulating pathogens.


The blue line indicates the R-values at a VoC proportion of 0%.


As a rule, with the emergence of VoC, the determined R-values (black line), deviate from the blue line and typically converge on the red line, becoming congruent the moment the selective sweep is complete and conventional variants have been fully replaced.

The turquoise lines in the diagram indicate the expected dynamics of future R-values since the Forecast start date.


Caution! If the forecast start date is set to more than 30 days in the past, you must adjust the forecast period in the control panel (the default is set to 30 days) for the present dynamics to remain visible.



Incidence Forecast


The black line indicates the number of new cases reported daily (same as the blue line under the incidence tab).


The fine grey lines that appear from the forecast start date are simulation-based predictions of future case numbers for the duration of the prediction interval (forecast length in days).


In the default setting, the tool runs five simulations with a forecast length of 30 days. Accordingly, five fine grey lines appear in the diagram and the forecasting period ends after 30 days.


In the control panel (left side), the number of runs and forecast length can be adjusted (182 days = ½ year, 274 days = ¾ year).


Green highlights show the percentile distribution for 95, 75, 25, and 5 percent. The green line corresponds to the median.


Caution! Forward predictions assume constant R-values. With long prediction intervals (forecast length), trends may be misleading, especially when days with more extreme R-values are chosen as the start date. For this reason, we suggest to select a day from the previous week with an R-value representative of the past seven-day interval as the start date.


Caution! If the computational demand is too high, e.g. nationwide computations with forecasts beyond 182 days (corresponding to half a year) and more than five runs, a warning (limiter) will be displayed and the values automatically return to the default setting.


Vaccination quota option: Number of people vaccinated per day (single dose) below the slider

This allows to make incidence predictions for any given vaccination rate.


In the number field, enter the expected number of vaccination doses administered per day for your selected catchment area.


The calculation is based on:

  • the daily increase in the number of vaccinated persons within the catchment area (assuming 50% of doses are 2nd vaccinations)

  • the proportion of vaccinated persons at the end of the prediction interval (forecast length in days)

  • the date until full (100% 2nd dose) vaccination of the entire catchment population, assuming constant vaccination rates.



Bed Forecast


The hospital bed forecast estimates the expected number Covid-19 patients in ICUs (red line) and normal wards (blue line). Colour shading indicates the percentile distributions of 95, 75, 25, and 5 percent. The lines correspond to the median.


The orange shaded field shows the proportion of newly recorded cases (previous day) requiring hospitalisation on the selected calendar day.


Intensive care shows the number of ICU beds occupied by Covid-19 patients as reported by the DIVI - Intensive Care Registry for the selected start date and the selected catchment area.


The number of Covid-19 patients on general wards is calculated using a model based on the average Covid 19 patient-care-path ascertained at the Medical Center - University of Freiburg (see publication in the tab About).


Manual adjustment of Covid-19-specific occupancy rates for ICU and general ward(s) improves prediction for individual hospitals!


Activating the check box “Show only ICU results” improves readability of the figure.


By activating the check box below, age-specific hospitalisation rates together with vaccine prioritisation of age-groups is taken into account and predictions thereby improved.


Tertiary care hospitals often admit patients directly to the ICU. This proportion can be adjusted by activating the appropriate function.

Parameter choices

The chosen parameters are based on the Covid-19-specific patient-care-path ascertained at the Medical Center - University of Freiburg (see also publication in the tab About).

We recommend to modify the default setting only if absolutely necessary.
