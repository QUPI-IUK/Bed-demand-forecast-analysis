Introduction


The Analysis and Prediction Tool for COVID-19 Cases can be reached via the following link:

https://iuk-forecast.uniklinik-freiburg.de/

The website opens in English by default.

A progress bar appears in the bottom right-hand corner of the screen (Fetching RKI data…).

After uploading the most recent data from

  • the Robert-Koch-Institut and
  • the Register of the German Interdisciplinary Association for Intensive Care and Emergency Medicine
    (DIVI Intensive Care Registry: Deutsche Interdisziplinäre Vereinigung für Intensiv- und Notfallmedizin, DIVI-Intensivregister),
the language will automatically switch to German. The application is now ready for use.

To change the language click on the Flag.

The user interface consists of a grey shaded control panel (on the left) and a results panel (on the right).

In the smartphone application, the panels are arranged in vertical order.

The results panel has eight tabs along the upper margin.

To run the analysis and prediction tool, we recommend using the first five tabs (from left to right), starting with Incidence.
In the following, we will introduce you to all functions in that order.


In the control panel (left side)

Choose a state from the drop down menu and enter the district(s) of the catchment area for which you wish to make a prediction (LK = Landkreis, SK = Stadtkreis). . Optionally, checking the box Select all states/Select all districts also allows for national or state-wide predictions.

The selected states and districts will be checked with a tick in the corresponding fields of the drop down menu.

  • Population shows the population size in the selected catchment area as reported by the authorities.
  • ICU bed occupancy (DIVI registry) provides the number of ICU beds currently occupied by Covid-19 patients in the selected areas.
  • The Forecast start date corresponds to the last calendar day for which RKI data are available (usually the previous day). This can be backdated as desired.
  • The Forecast length (days) and the Number of runs can be set and selectively changed. In the default settings, the forecast length is set to 30 days and the number of simulations to five runs. The limits of these functions are 365 days and 100 runs.

In the results panel (right side):

The result of your search will appear. The blue line in the graph represents the epidemiological curve of cases reported for each calendar day. The scale is on the left y-axes.
The black line illustrates the seven-day incidence (per 100,000 inhabitants) for each calendar day. The scale is on the right y-axes.

By using the slider beneath the chart, calendar periods can be narrowed down to improve resolution and visibility.

The blue field beneath the slider shows the number of newly reported infections.

The grey field beneath the slider shows the current seven-day incidence (per 100.000).



The resulting figure illustrates the time-dependent effective R-value for each calendar day, including the 95% in grey shade bordered by a stippled line.

The vertical grey line corresponds to the date of the most recent R-value (previous day). This value is also rounded to two decimal points in the grey field.

To compensate for the RKI-dependent reporting delay, a Nowcast R-value is specified. This serves as the baseline for the forward predictions.

The turquoise lines in the diagram indicate the expected dynamics of future R-values since the Forecast start date.

By changing the Forecast start date in the control panel (left side), the effective R-value for each previous calendar day can be called up. This date is then indicated by a vertical ochre line in the progression chart and in the field with the same colour underneath (set the slider accordingly).

Option (right lower corner)::

Using the exponential smoothing function allow for an illustration of the uncertainty of predicting future effective R-values. It displays likely trajectories as turquoise lines based on the variation of the effective R-values over the past 100 days. The number of trajectories corresponds to the Number of Run set in the control panel. Include the effect of Variants of Concern (VoC) in the right lower corner.
Activating this function allows to predict the displacement dynamics of VoC.
Enter a historical date for which the proportion of VoC is known.
In the next field, enter the proportion of VoC determined on that date.
In the bottom field, enter the relative fitness advantage of the VoC (relative increase in transmissibility). In the default setting, the relative fitness advantage is set to 50%.

In the figure a red line appears above and a blue line below the recorded R-value estimates (black line).

The red line indicates the expected R-values, were the VoC proportion to make up 100% of the circulating pathogens.

The blue line indicates the R-values at a VoC proportion of 0%.

As a rule, with the emergence of VoC, the determined R-values (black line), deviate from the blue line and typically converge on the red line, becoming congruent the moment the selective sweep is complete and conventional variants have been fully replaced.

Note: the results panel has an export button that allows to download the illustrated results as numerical values in a *.csv format



The graph in the results section illustrates the proportion (%) of the vaccinated population in the selected catchment over time.

The area in bright green shows the population who received the first dose, and in dark green those with a second dose.

The dashed line increasing over time illustrates the proportion of the population with an immunity that protects from potential infectiousness, i.e. those who cannot transmit anymore to others. In the default setting dominance of the Delta-variant is assumed and levels of protection against infectiousness are set at 25% after the first dose and 55% after the second dose. With newly emerging variants, these variables need to be adjusted accordingly (see tab Parameters)

The vertical dashed line indicates the Forecast start date and the number of started immunisations (first dose administrations) on that day are shown in the data field below. This number can be modified for optional scenario predictions.

For first vaccinations, the forecast assumes a constant increase, based on the vaccination rate of the Forecast start date (represented by a 7-day moving average).

The forecast for the second vaccination takes the delay between the first and second dose on the Forecast start date into account and keeps this delay constant for the projection.

Values in bright and dark green shaded data fields report the proportion vaccinated on the start date and predict that proportion for the end of the forecast period for first and second dose vaccines.



The black line indicates the number of new cases reported daily (same as the blue line under the incidence tab).

The grey lines that project from the Forecast start date represent simulations based on the trajectories of effective R-values in the Eff.R (EpiEstim) tab. Orange shaded areas describe the probability distribution in percentiles of 95, 75, 25, and 5 percent for future case numbers for the Forecast length (days). The middle line corresponds to the median.

In the control panel (left side), the number of runs and Forecast length (days) can be adjusted.

Option: Simulate weekly reporting pattern:
By activating this option, predictions will follow the historical weekly reporting pattern. This function allows for a more realistic illustration of the daily-expected number of cases.

Caution!
With long prediction intervals trends may be misleading. As a rule predictions that exceed more than 14 day should be taken with caution and become increasingly unreliable after 30 days. The model cannot anticipate changes in contact habits within the population (such as seasonal effects, school holidays, mass-gatherings, and non-pharmaceutical interventions).



The hospital bed forecast estimates the expected number Covid-19 patients in ICUs (red line) and general wards (blue line). Colour shading indicates the percentile distributions of 95, 75, 25, and 5 percent. The lines correspond to the median.

The orange shaded field shows the proportion of newly recorded cases (previous day) that required hospitalisation on the selected Forecast start date.

The data field Intensive care shows the number of ICU beds occupied by Covid-19 patients as reported by the DIVI - Intensive Care Registry for the selected start date and the selected catchment area.

The number of Covid-19 patients on general wards is calculated using a model based on the average Covid 19 patient-care-path ascertained at the Medical Center - University of Freiburg (see publication in the tab About).

Manual adjustment of Covid-19-specific occupancy rates for ICU and general ward(s) improves prediction for individual hospitals!

Activating the check box “Show only ICU results” improves readability of the figure.

Tertiary care hospitals often admit patients directly to the ICU. This proportion can be adjusted by activating the appropriate function.

Validation of Bed Forecast (highly recommended):

For all historical dates the observed daily bed-occupancy is displayed as full dots until the Forecast start date. When forecasts (e.g. for validity testing) are intended for historical calendar dates by resetting the date in the window Select start data point, observed bed-occupancy will remain visible as small circles and superimposed by the historical prediction from the selected start point.

If validation of the predictions is desired, a file with the historical occupancy rates should be uploaded under the browse function.

Parameter choices

The selection of parameters (serial interval, expected vaccine efficacy, distributions and standard diviations) are based on most recent accepted knowledge and will be updated accordingly. Date of recent update and reference publication are provided.
The average duration of stay in normal wards and ICU are based on the Covid-19-specific patient-care-path ascertained at the Medical Center - University of Freiburg (see also publication in the tab "About").
We recommend to modify the default setting only if necessary in order to adjust for individual health care settings.

GW = General Ward
ICU = Intensive Care Unit
SD = Step Down Unit
